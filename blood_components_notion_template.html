Blood components

HP&L reference — functions, aviation risks, and treatment

Blood cells

🔴

Red blood cells

Erythrocytes

Oxygen transport
ContainsHaemoglobin
Lifespan~120 days
Produced inBone marrow
ShapeBiconcave disc
CO binds to haemoglobin 200x stronger than O2 — causes anaemic hypoxia
⬜

White blood cells

Leucocytes

Immune defense
DetectsBacteria, viruses
ActionAttack and destroy
Also createsAntibodies
Active infection = not fit to fly. Even a cold affects ears, sinuses, and concentration
🟡

Platelets

Thrombocytes

Blood clotting
TriggerDamaged vessel
ActionForm clot plug
Produced inBone marrow
Blood thinners reduce platelet function — may ground a pilot. Always declare medication

Other components

💧

Plasma

~90% water

Liquid carrier
CarriesAll blood cells
Also carriesNutrients, hormones
Also carriesAntibodies, waste
Dehydration thickens blood — impairs O2 delivery, raises DVT risk, degrades cognition
🫀

Haemoglobin

Inside red blood cells

O2 binding protein
Normal bindingHaemoglobin + O2
Abnormal bindingHaemoglobin + CO
CO bond strength200x stronger than O2
Low haemoglobin = anaemia = anaemic hypoxia. CO poisoning is odourless and colourless

Bleeding types

Type Colour Flow Danger Treatment
Arterial Bright red Pulsating / spurting Most dangerous Immediate firm pressure + tourniquet + 999
Venous Dark red Steady slow flow Serious Firm pressure + elevate wound
Capillary Red Slow oozing Least dangerous Light pressure + clean dressing

Oxygen transport chain

Air breathed in → lungs extract O2
↓
O2 binds to haemoglobin inside red blood cells
↓
Plasma carries red blood cells through bloodstream
↓
O2 delivered to all body tissues
↓
CO2 collected as waste and returned to lungs
↓
Disruption anywhere = hypoxia

Memory tricks

Quick recall

🔴 Red = Respiration (oxygen transport)
⬜ White = Warfare (immune defense)
🟡 Yellow = Yield bleeding (clotting)
💧 Plasma = Pipeline (carries everything)
🫀 Haemo = Highway for oxygen

Bleeding colours

Bright red + spurting = arterial (fight for your life!)
Dark red + flowing = venous (still dangerous)
Any red + oozing = capillary (easy to manage)
