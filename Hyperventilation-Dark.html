Hyperventilation

HP&L reference — definition, causes, chemistry, symptoms, and countermeasures

Definition

Hyperventilation is breathing too fast or too deep, causing excessive CO2 to be expelled from the body, creating a dangerous chemical imbalance in the blood.

NOT an oxygen problem — it is a CO2 imbalance problem!!

The chemistry — why it causes symptoms

Breathing too fast or too deep
↓
Too much CO2 expelled from lungs
↓
Blood CO2 levels drop (hypocapnia)
↓
Blood pH rises — becomes too alkaline
↓
Blood vessels constrict throughout body
↓
Less calcium available to muscles = tetany/spasms
↓
Less blood reaching brain = dizziness, confusion, unconsciousness

Two KEY identifying symptoms — unique to hyperventilation!!

Tingling fingers and lips

The most important identifying symptom!! If a pilot experiences tingling in the fingers or lips = hyperventilation, NOT hypoxia. This symptom does NOT occur in hypoxia!!

Tetany — muscle spasms

Muscle spasms and lockup caused by CO2 loss reducing calcium availability. This is UNIQUE to hyperventilation — never occurs in hypoxia. Many students incorrectly assign this to hypoxia!!

Countermeasures

MethodHowWhy it works
Primary
Slow breathing down		 Conscious effort to breathe slowly and deeply Allows CO2 levels to rebuild in blood
Secondary
Paper bag method		 Breathe in and out of paper bag Rebreathes expelled CO2 — restores blood balance
Alternative
Talk out loud		 Read checklist aloud, talk to ATC Forces slower breathing pattern naturally
If unsure
Use oxygen		 Put on supplemental oxygen immediately Rules out hypoxia — treat most dangerous first!!
Always
Remove cause		 Reduce anxiety, pain, heat if possible Eliminates the trigger causing fast breathing

The dangerous overlap with hypoxia

Both conditions share these symptoms — making diagnosis difficult!!

Dizziness — shared by both
Confusion — shared by both
Headache — shared by both
Impaired judgement — shared by both
Unconsciousness if untreated — shared by both

Golden rule when unsure:

Always treat for HYPOXIA first!! Hypoxia is more immediately fatal. Put on oxygen AND slow your breathing — this covers both conditions simultaneously!!

Memory tricks

Unique hyperventilation symptoms — never forget these!!

Tingling fingers/lips = HYPERVENTILATION only!!
Muscle spasms/tetany = HYPERVENTILATION only!!
Rapid breathing = the CAUSE not just a symptom!!

The core difference

Hypoxia = TOO LITTLE oxygen reaching tissues
Hyperventilation = TOO MUCH CO2 expelled from blood
Hypoxia = oxygen problem
Hyperventilation = CO2 balance problem!!

Treatment memory

SLOW DOWN breathing = primary treatment
Paper BAG = rebreathes CO2 back in
TALK aloud = forces slower breathing
If UNSURE = treat hypoxia first!!
