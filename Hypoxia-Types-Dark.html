Hypoxia — definition and types

HP&L reference — HASH types, symptoms, TUC, and countermeasures

Definition

Hypoxia is an insufficient supply of oxygen reaching the body tissues, impairing normal function of the brain and organs.

"Hypo" = under/insufficient — "Oxia" = oxygen

The four types — HASH

H

Hypoxic

Most common in aviation!!

Not enough O2 in the air
CauseReduced O2 partial pressure at altitude
OnsetAbove 10,000ft
Night visionAffected from 5,000ft!!
At 18,000ft pressure is HALVED — only half the O2 available per breath
A

Anaemic

Blood can't carry O2

Haemoglobin problem
Cause 1CO poisoning
Cause 2Anaemia (low haemoglobin)
Cause 3Blood loss
CO binds to haemoglobin 200x stronger than O2 — colourless and odourless!!
S

Stagnant

Blood not circulating

Circulation problem
Cause 1G-forces (blood pools in legs)
Cause 2Heart problems
Cause 3Shock or tight clothing
O2 in blood is fine but blood not reaching tissues — leads to grey-out and G-LOC
H

Histotoxic

Cells can't use O2

Cellular problem
Cause 1Alcohol poisoning
Cause 2Drugs / narcotics
Cause 3Cyanide poisoning
O2 delivered perfectly but cells REFUSE to use it — explains why alcohol is so dangerous for pilots

Symptoms by severity

Mild — above 10,000ft

Euphoria / feeling great (most dangerous!!)
False sense of wellbeing
Slight impairment of judgement
Night vision deteriorates

Moderate — 15,000–20,000ft

Headache and dizziness
Visual impairment
Impaired judgement and decisions
Muscle weakness
Cyanosis begins (blue lips/nails)

Severe — above 20,000ft

Severe confusion
Loss of muscle control
Tunnel vision
Loss of consciousness
Death if untreated

Time of useful consciousness (TUC)

AltitudeTUCRisk level
10,000ftIndefinite (with mild symptoms)Low
25,000ft3–5 minutesHigh
30,000ft1–2 minutesCritical
35,000ft30–60 secondsCritical
40,000ft15–20 secondsFatal without action

Countermeasures

Recognise symptoms early — especially euphoria!!
↓
Descend immediately below 10,000ft
↓
Use supplemental oxygen immediately
↓
Pressurised cabin — maintain below 8,000ft equivalent
↓
Know your TUC at cruise altitude before every flight

Most dangerous symptom

Euphoria — the silent killer

Pilot feels GREAT while oxygen is dangerously low. No urge to take corrective action. This is why hypoxia kills — the pilot never realises anything is wrong until it is too late.

Memory tricks

HASH — the four types

H — Hypoxic = not enough O2 in the AIR (altitude!)
A — Anaemic = blood can't CARRY O2 (CO poisoning!)
S — Stagnant = blood not MOVING (G-forces!)
H — Histotoxic = cells can't USE O2 (alcohol/drugs!)

Quick symptom recall

Euphoria = mild hypoxia (most dangerous — feels fine!!)
Cyanosis = blue lips/nails = moderate hypoxia
Unconscious = severe hypoxia — act NOW!!
Night vision affected from just 5,000ft!!
